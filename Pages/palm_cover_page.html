Palm Cover

Project Problem Statement

Design a mechanism that aids a client, affected by Generalized dystonia, with her fine motor skills directed towards writing, drawing, and painting.

As a result of this condition, the client experiences shaking and pain in their right hand and wrist when writing or typing for prolonged time periods.

This system will be accurate, durable and comfortable enough to be used in any location.


Project Objective and Constraints

The overarching function of our design was to allow the client to write, draw, and paint comfortably and accurately in any location.


The resulting primary objectives of this device were for it be portable and accurate.

    The portability sub-objective included, making the device lightweight and small.

    The accuracy sub-objective, included ensuring that it was sturdy, comfortable, and adjustable.


Some constraints to the design were:

    The mechanism could not be too light due to the effects that the clientâ€™s dystonia has on the stability of her hands.

    The mechanism must be resistant to liquid due to allow the client to paint, and use the product in wet conditions without cauing any damage to its structure.

    Due to the limited budget, the design must cost less than fifty dollars to construct.

Planning

Stage 1 - Ideation

Palm Cover

First of Three Preliminary Designs

This design made use of a brace which would take on the client's natural handwriting form went on to be the selected design for its ability to best meet the previouly mentioned objectives and constraints.

Flat Glove and Ball

Second of Three Preliminary Designs

This design made use of a glove and a ball which brought up concerns regarding the stability of the utensil and ultimately the design was not ideal.

Ball and Frame

Third of Three Preliminary Designs

This design was a stable moving grid with wires and a similar ball as the second design to hold the writing utensil in place but the design proved to be too cumbersome to use and was rejected.

Stage 2 - Low-Fidelity Prototypes

To further affirm the validity of the 2 selected design ideas, low-fidelity prototypes were created of each.

Flat Glove and Ball

The low-fidelity prototype of the Flat Glove and Ball reconfirmed our concerns about the stability of the utensil attached to the ball and the idea was therefore rejected.

Palm Cover

The low-fidelity prototype of the Palm Cover performed very well and produced very positive reviews and an initial prototype.

As such the Palm Cover was deemed the design of choice and went on to the second stage of prototyping.

Stage 3 - Medium-Fidelity Prototype

Improved Design Sketch

Based on the critisism recieved with the low-fidelity prototype, slight modifications and adjustments were made to improve the design.

Medium-Fidelity Prototype

A medium-fidelity prototype of the palm cover was created using higher grade materials and a more sturdy structure.

Stage 4 - Final Prototype

Computer Aided Design

Using Inventor AutoCAD, the base of the brace was designed such that it would be sturdy yet lightweight.

3D Printed Base

Using the CAD drawing, the base of the brace was successfully 3D printed using Polylactic acid

The base would provide the sturdy structure yet an ample amount of weight in the hand to withstand the side effects of the client's Generalized dystonia.

Base with Sponge

After obtaining the base, the padding made with a sponge and the fastener made with velco were secured to the base.

The sponge will provide additional comfort to the clientâ€™s hand despite clenching the Palm Cover for a long duration and the velco straps will allow the mechanism to remain strapped to her hand and also allow the utensil of her choice (Regardless of the size of utensil) to remain secure.

Final Prototype

After the base was padded and the velco was fastened, the entire mechanism was covered in a shower curtain.

The shower curtain would serve two purposes, the first would be to prevent any water from danaging the Palm Cover, effectively making it waterproof. Additionally it would reduce surface friction so the chances of the client feeling discomfort as a result of rashes from prolonged use would be minimized.

The Final Prototype had successfully met previouly defined objectives and cleared all the limitations.
