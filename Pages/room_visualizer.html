Room Visualizer

General Description

The spatial measurement system acquires information about the area around the system based upon the press of a single button.

The system operates in 3 stages:

    Using a stepping motor to provide a 360 degree measurement of the distance along a single vertical (y-z) geometric plane, with multiple measurements taken at different distances along the x axis.

    Upon obtaining the data of the surrounding environment, the information is stored on the microcontroller’s local memory which is communicated to the user’s computer.

    After successful communication, the computer will then take the data and process it into a graphic for the user to see.

Physical Components

The physical result of the circuit schematic:

The Time of Flight sensor (Used to determine the distance of the closest object by firing a laser and calculating the distance travelled by the laser based on the time it took to bounce back to the sensor)

The stepper motor which rotates the ToF sensor 360 degrees to obtain a distance reading of a single yz-plane which is activaded by the button on the breadboard

The compact box used to house the device is moved 10cm forward along the x-axis while keeping everything steady and after taking a single 360 degree scan the box moves again to obtain the measurements of the next position on the x-axis.
